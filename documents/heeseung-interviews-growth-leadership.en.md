# Heeseung Interview Excerpts: Growth, Leadership, And Self-Understanding

## 201227

Q: In the survey before ENHYPEN's leader selection, for questions like "the person you most want to see when things are hard" and "the person who takes the lead in doing things they do not like," HEESEUNG's name was always near the top.

A: I am someone who listens well to other people's worries. Saying it this way makes me sound a little self-satisfied. If it is something I can help them solve, I cannot really stand seeing someone be troubled by it. I get the thought, "I definitely have to help them," and it becomes something I do unconsciously.

Q: It is clear that the other members trust and rely on older brother Heeseung. In daily life, what kind of older brother do you want to be?

A: I do not want everyone to think of me as an important older brother. After spending time in places like middle school, high school, and clubs, I felt that older people are not always easy to approach. So when I became part of a group with the members, I wanted the atmosphere for conversation to be relaxed. To the younger members, I am not some great older brother. I think I should become a cute older brother. If I am that kind of older brother, everyone can talk comfortably.

Q: In I-LAND, Heeseung's older brother said, "When I think of the past you, the ordinary, immature, cute you who liked playing games with friends and did not like studying, I am so proud of who you are now."

A: Back then, I really was immature. Without exaggeration, everything has changed. This is a bit of TMI, but I have an old friend. When I went out with that friend, we would do a lot of quirky things together and live very innocently. I have matured a lot compared with then. Although I think I still need to become even more mature than I am now.

Q: In the "ENHYPEN October Month-End Settlement" video, you set a goal to practice vocals for one and a half to two hours every day, and even with a busy schedule, you achieved 70% to 80% of that goal.

A: When you are doing something you like, reaching a goal is easy. I usually put all of my energy into the things I like, but I do not have motivation for things I dislike. I think that is both a strength and a weakness. Taking studying as an example, in middle school I lost interest in studying, so I did not care much about most subjects. But I really liked English because I liked listening to pop songs. There was a high school near my home that admitted students only based on their English grades, and my English grades had already reached the admission standard.

## 210427

MTV News: Heeseung, you previously said, "The more time you spend on work, the less time you invest in yourself." How do you divide your time between work and investing in yourself?

Heeseung: These days, most of my time is spent on work, but focusing on work is also part of investing in myself. I do not think those two things are different.

## 211021

GQ: Connect, discover, grow. These three words symbolize ENHYPEN. What would you like to say about them recently?

Heeseung: I think growing over time and maintaining close connections with people are very important, so I especially want to point out the word "discover." The process of discovering myself and finding new things is my recent interest.

GQ: What have you discovered?

Heeseung: It is very simple. I just feel that my original thoughts have become more important. It is a very simple, ordinary thing.

## 211222

Q: Heeseung is the oldest member in ENHYPEN. What do you think are the strengths and weaknesses of your personality?

Heeseung: "If someone is someone I want to cherish, I will do my best to treat them well. I think that is a good side of me. My weakness is that there are too few people who can reach that level for me."

## 220119

Q: At the Mnet Asia Music Awards ceremony, it felt like you had become more confident.

Heeseung: Yes. In the past, I thought speaking in front of others was something very stressful, but this kind of problem cannot be solved just by thinking about it. I need to put in more effort. Now, when I talk about myself, I do not feel as awkward as before. It feels comfortable to immediately communicate the thoughts I have with other people.

Q: But on your personal profile for MBC's Weekly Idol, you wrote that you have "many" strengths, and that your weakness is "having no weaknesses."

Heeseung: I thought that if I wrote that I had no weaknesses, maybe it could become a way to raise my confidence. Rather than looking at my weaknesses, I would rather focus on my strengths. It would be best if the infinite possibilities I have move in the direction I want, and then I can turn my weaknesses into strengths. Changing your mindset might be simple, but it can bring a big change. For me, holding that kind of attitude can solve more problems.

## 220712

Q: The practice process must be tiring. As the older brother, is there anything you want to say?

Heeseung: Actually, I have not used my role as the oldest member to say anything. There is no need to insist on saying something either. I am young too, and I just happened to become the oldest member. There is no real difference between me and the members. Although I do give them strength, there are also many things I need to learn from the younger members. If I did something just because I am the older brother, the younger members would feel uncomfortable too.

Q: Looking at it that way, have you become more professional about work?

Heeseung: I think I have become more constructive. In the early days after debut, I was among many people, and if I only thought emotionally, nothing could be solved. Because I wanted to approach and judge things more logically, that kind of personality developed. I can construct ideas and approach them through work, which leads to results like cover songs. It seems that through many attempts, that side of my personality came out.

## 220823

Q: You did well. When ENHYPEN was deciding on a leader, your name kept coming up, and you also took the leader test. Was that also because you simply wanted to be a comfortable older brother to the members?

A: The members said it would be good if the older brother led us. But honestly, even if I were not the leader, I could still do that. This is the first time I am saying this, but there was a situation like that. If I became the leader, I worried that even if I could not notice problems in myself, I might still guide the team according to my own thoughts. I also worried that I might lose my original self that way. I was very worried that this kind of result could happen. If that happened, because there is an age gap between us, reducing disagreements would not be easy. So I worried about it for a long time, and after thinking about it, I felt that watching over the members and bringing them together as one whole was my role.

Q: You are very good at self-objectification. Starting in the 2020s, it seems hard to distinguish between the "ideal me" and the "actual me," and even the "me under criticism" becomes something to question.

A: I do not understand myself that well either. In fact, I thought I was a very strong person, but recently it seems I have become a very emotional person. There is also a part of me that wants to receive love. I was originally the youngest child at home, so I think I have that side.

Q: When did you feel that?

A: On stage. When I do something, I have the expectation of hoping there will be more response.

Q: Is that not an expectation that strong people can have too?

A: Yes. But compared with confidence, that state is more about wanting to receive love. The expectations I had fulfilled by my family can also be satisfied in other situations. I realized, "Ah, I really am someone who has that side too," and I learned a lot.
